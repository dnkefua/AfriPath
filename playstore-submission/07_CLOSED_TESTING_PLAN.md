# Closed Testing Plan

## Track

- Track type: Google Play Closed testing
- Recommended tester recruitment target: 20-30 testers
- Minimum for production access on newer personal developer accounts: 12 opted-in testers
- Minimum opt-in duration before production access request: 14 continuous days

## Tester Group Setup

- Preferred setup: Google Group or managed email list
- Tester email requirement: Gmail or Google Workspace account
- Status: NEEDS MANUAL ACTION in Play Console

## Tester Instructions

Send testers the Play closed testing opt-in link and ask them to:

1. Opt in and remain opted in for at least 14 continuous days.
2. Install AfriPath on an Android phone.
3. Open the app at least three times during the test period.
4. Test Home, Search, opportunity detail pages, Visa Hub, Command Center, Saved, privacy, and support links.
5. Save at least one opportunity and track one application workflow.
6. Report any crashes, layout issues, broken links, confusing eligibility language, or missing opportunities.
7. Submit feedback through Google Play private feedback or the developer feedback channel.

## Feedback Questions

- Was the app's purpose clear within the first minute?
- Were opportunity cards easy to distinguish and scan?
- Were scholarship, job, volunteer, and visa pathway filters useful?
- Were international applicant, work permit, sponsorship, or relocation signals clear?
- Did source links open correctly?
- Did the Command Center help you understand next steps?
- Did any Android screen look broken, crowded, or hard to read?
- What opportunity category should AfriPath add more of before production?

## Production Access Evidence To Collect

- Screenshot of closed testing track tester count.
- Screenshot showing at least 12 opted-in testers.
- Evidence that testers remained opted in for 14 continuous days.
- List of app versions tested.
- Summary of feedback themes.
- Bug fixes or UX improvements made from tester feedback.
- Crash/pre-launch report notes.

## Production Access Draft Answer

AfriPath was tested with a controlled group of Android testers representative of students, professionals, job seekers, and volunteers exploring global opportunities. Testers were asked to exercise the full closed-testing build, including search, opportunity details, saved items, Visa Hub, Command Center, readiness tools, source links, privacy, and support pages. Feedback was collected through Play private feedback and the developer feedback channel, then grouped by usability, content clarity, source quality, Android layout, and missing opportunity categories.
